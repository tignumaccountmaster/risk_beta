Excursion — Business Case

Human Performance Beta as a risk management instrument.

Risk management means identifying and analysing risk in an investment — and every project a client runs is an investment. It evaluates both upsides (gains) and downsides (leaks) against project goals. At its core, risk is the probability of a loss, measured using statistical methods. One of those methods is called Beta.

A leader can have deep functional expertise and a well-defined role — but if the Beta is below 1.0, both are diminished. The Beta is not a wellness score. It is the multiplier on everything else a leader brings to a critical moment.

In finance

Beta measures volatility
relative to the market

β = 1 The market. The benchmark. Exactly average volatility. All investments are measured against this.
β > 1 More volatile than the market. Higher potential return — but higher risk of loss.
β < 1 Less volatile than the market. Lower risk — but also lower upside potential.

The two risk types: Systematic risk affects everyone — a pandemic, a financial crisis, a market shock. Unsystematic risk is specific to a company or team — poor leadership, misaligned culture, performance drain.

The TIGNUM inversion

Human Performance Beta
runs the other way

β = 1 The performance benchmark. The minimum standard for a team entering a critical project.
β > 1 Multiplying. The team generates more capacity than it consumes. This is where you want to be.
β < 1 Diminishing. The team is a performance liability. Lower beta = higher risk to the project.

The inversion is intentional and important. In finance, you avoid high beta if you want safety. In human performance, you pursue high beta. A CFO can hold both concepts simultaneously — they are measuring the same underlying thing (risk relative to a benchmark) from opposite directions.

Readiness within the team and the project

Unsystematic risk is specific to a team or organisation — it can be reduced through deliberate action. This is where TIGNUM operates most directly.

The instrument captures this through energy multiplication, performance resilience, and self-observation behaviours — the dimensions that determine whether a team amplifies or drains collective capacity under project pressure.

The benchmark

A Prindex score of 62.5%
equals a Beta of 1.0.

A Prindex score of 62.5% is the expert-defined performance standard — the minimum readiness level for a team entering a critical project. It is not a statistical average. It is a professional judgment about what the work actually requires.

Under this standard, only 13.1% of leaders currently meet or exceed the benchmark on their first assessment. This is intentional — it sets a demanding, aspirational standard that reflects the genuine requirements of high-stakes leadership.

03 — Methodology

Five steps from answer to Beta.

The calculation is deterministic, transparent, and reproducible across all three assessment generations spanning 2011 to today.

I
Pillar Score
avg(answers per pillar)

Mean of all answers mapped to each Level × Pillar combination. Double-mapped questions contribute to both buckets independently.

II
Level Score
(M + N + Mv + R) / 4

Average of four pillar scores — Mindset, Nutrition, Movement, Recovery — for each level. All four carry equal weight within a level.

III
Overall Score
(1×BP + 2×DC + 3×CM) / 6

Weighted average. Critical Moments carries 3× weight. Divisor 6 is the sum of weights (1+2+3), keeping the result on the 1–5 scale.

IV
Raw Beta
Score / 2.5

Maps the 1–5 score onto a 0–2 beta axis. Score 1.0 → β 0.40. Score 2.5 → β 1.00. Score 5.0 → β 2.00.

V
Normalised Beta
Raw Beta × 0.9259

Anchored to the median of 38,686 pre-assessments (42.0% → Raw β 1.080 → × 0.9259 = 1.000). By definition, the median first-assessment beta equals 1.0. The threshold is derived from the data, not assumed.

Brain & Physiology
Foundational readiness — weight 1×

Sleep, nutrition, movement, recovery habits. The biological substrate everything else runs on. Necessary but not sufficient — a leader can sleep well and still collapse under negotiation pressure.

Daily Challenges
Operational readiness — weight 2×

Performance under continuous load. How the leader shows up across the unrelenting sequence of decisions, meetings, and transitions that define the executive work day.

Critical Moments
Peak performance capacity — weight 3×

Readiness for high-stakes negotiations, board presentations, and decisions where the outcome disproportionately determines enterprise value. The moments that separate ordinary from extraordinary.

05 — Physiological Anchoring

The Beta measures something real.

The central question about any behavioural self-report instrument is whether it tracks actual physiological reality or just how someone wishes they lived. A subset of 517 leaders completed comprehensive medical assessments alongside their SHPI assessments — blood biomarkers, cardiovascular screening, body composition, and functional movement testing. The correlations answer the question directly.

Leaders with higher movement scores have measurably higher aerobic capacity. Leaders with higher nutrition scores have measurably lower blood pressure and smaller waist circumference. Behaviour predicts physiology — and physiology validates behaviour. The instrument is not measuring aspiration. It is measuring adaptation.

06 — Technical Reference

Calculating the Beta in the database.

The SQL below retrieves individual normalised betas from prindex_result. The normalisation factor (0.9259) is fixed — derived from the pre-assessment population median and stored as a constant so the threshold does not drift as new data accumulates.

Corrected SQL — returns individual betas per leader
-- Normalisation factor: fixed constant derived from 38,686 pre-assessments
-- Pre-assessment median score = 2.700  →  raw beta = 1.080  →  factor = 1/1.080 = 0.9259
-- DO NOT recalculate from live data — threshold must remain stable

SELECT
  assessment.id,
  assessment.user_id,
  users.email,
  user_profile.company_name,
  assessment.completed_at,
  assessment.rn                                               AS survey_number,
  assessment.overall_score,
  ROUND(assessment.overall_score / 2.5, 4)                   AS raw_beta,
  ROUND(assessment.overall_score / 2.5 * 0.9259, 4)            AS norm_beta,
  CASE
    WHEN ROUND(assessment.overall_score / 2.5 * 0.9259, 4) >= 1.0
      THEN 'Multiplying'
    ELSE 'Diminishing'
  END                                                     AS beta_state

FROM (
  SELECT
    prindex_result.id,
    prindex_result.user_id,
    prindex_result.completed_at,
    ROW_NUMBER() OVER (
      PARTITION BY prindex_result.user_id
      ORDER BY prindex_result.completed_at
    )                                                     AS rn,
    (prindex_result.summary #>> '{score}'::text[])::double precision
                                                          AS overall_score
  FROM prindex_result
  WHERE prindex_result.state = 'COMPLETED'
) AS assessment

LEFT JOIN users        ON users.id = assessment.user_id
LEFT JOIN user_profile ON user_profile.user_id = users.id

WHERE assessment.rn = 1                                       -- pre-assessment only
  AND users.email NOT LIKE '%tignum%'               -- exclude test accounts
  AND ({{email}})
  AND ({{company_name}})
  AND ({{Survey_Date}})

ORDER BY assessment.completed_at DESC
Team summary — one row per company, pre-assessments only

Aggregates all leaders within a company to produce a single team beta, the percentage multiplying, and the count. Filters to first assessments only so the result reflects the entry-point baseline — where the team sits before any engagement.

-- Team Beta: average normalised beta for all first-assessment leaders in a company
-- Returns one row per company with team beta, state split, and member count

SELECT
  user_profile.company_name,
  COUNT(*)                                                    AS n_leaders,
  ROUND(AVG(assessment.norm_beta), 4)                            AS team_beta,
  ROUND(AVG(assessment.norm_beta), 4) >= 1.0                    AS team_multiplying,
  ROUND(
    SUM(CASE WHEN assessment.norm_beta >= 1.0 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)::numeric
    / COUNT(*) * 100, 1
  )                                                           AS pct_multiplying,
  ROUND(
    SUM(CASE WHEN assessment.norm_beta < 1.0 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)::numeric
    / COUNT(*) * 100, 1
  )                                                           AS pct_diminishing,
  MIN(assessment.completed_at)                                    AS earliest_survey,
  MAX(assessment.completed_at)                                    AS latest_survey

FROM (
  SELECT
    prindex_result.user_id,
    prindex_result.completed_at,
    ROUND(
      (prindex_result.summary #>> '{score}'::text[])::double precision
      / 2.5 * 0.9259, 4
    )                                                         AS norm_beta,
    ROW_NUMBER() OVER (
      PARTITION BY prindex_result.user_id
      ORDER BY prindex_result.completed_at
    )                                                         AS rn
  FROM prindex_result
  WHERE prindex_result.state = 'COMPLETED'
) AS assessment

LEFT JOIN users        ON users.id = assessment.user_id
LEFT JOIN user_profile ON user_profile.user_id = users.id

WHERE assessment.rn = 1                                           -- pre-assessment only
  AND users.email NOT LIKE '%tignum%'
  AND ({{company_name}})
  AND ({{Survey_Date}})

GROUP BY user_profile.company_name
ORDER BY team_beta ASC
Team pre / post movement — risk reduction per company

For leaders who have completed more than one assessment, this compares their first (pre) beta against their most recent (post) beta and aggregates to team level. Risk reduction = (post − pre) ÷ post.

-- Team pre/post: first vs most recent assessment per user, aggregated by company
-- Only includes users with 2+ completed assessments

WITH assessments AS (
  SELECT
    prindex_result.user_id,
    prindex_result.completed_at,
    ROUND(
      (prindex_result.summary #>> '{score}'::text[])::double precision
      / 2.5 * 0.9259, 4
    )                                                         AS norm_beta,
    ROW_NUMBER() OVER (
      PARTITION BY prindex_result.user_id
      ORDER BY prindex_result.completed_at ASC
    )                                                         AS rn_asc,
    ROW_NUMBER() OVER (
      PARTITION BY prindex_result.user_id
      ORDER BY prindex_result.completed_at DESC
    )                                                         AS rn_desc,
    COUNT(*) OVER (PARTITION BY prindex_result.user_id)      AS total_surveys
  FROM prindex_result
  WHERE prindex_result.state = 'COMPLETED'
),
paired AS (
  SELECT
    pre.user_id,
    pre.norm_beta                                             AS pre_beta,
    post.norm_beta                                            AS post_beta,
    post.norm_beta - pre.norm_beta                           AS beta_change,
    pre.completed_at                                          AS pre_date,
    post.completed_at                                         AS post_date,
    EXTRACT(DAY FROM post.completed_at - pre.completed_at)   AS days_gap
  FROM assessments pre
  JOIN assessments post ON post.user_id = pre.user_id
  WHERE pre.rn_asc = 1
    AND post.rn_desc = 1
    AND pre.total_surveys > 1
    AND pre.rn_asc != post.rn_desc  -- ensure pre != post row
)

SELECT
  user_profile.company_name,
  COUNT(*)                                                    AS n_leaders,
  ROUND(AVG(paired.pre_beta), 4)                             AS pre_team_beta,
  ROUND(AVG(paired.post_beta), 4)                            AS post_team_beta,
  ROUND(AVG(paired.beta_change), 4)                          AS avg_beta_change,
  ROUND(
    (AVG(paired.post_beta) - AVG(paired.pre_beta))
    / AVG(paired.post_beta) * 100, 1
  )                                                           AS risk_reduction_pct,
  SUM(CASE WHEN paired.beta_change > 0 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)   AS n_improved,
  ROUND(
    SUM(CASE WHEN paired.beta_change > 0 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)::numeric
    / COUNT(*) * 100, 1
  )                                                           AS pct_improved,
  ROUND(AVG(paired.days_gap), 0)                             AS avg_days_between

FROM paired
LEFT JOIN users        ON users.id = paired.user_id
LEFT JOIN user_profile ON user_profile.user_id = users.id

WHERE users.email NOT LIKE '%tignum%'
  AND ({{company_name}})

GROUP BY user_profile.company_name
ORDER BY risk_reduction_pct DESC
